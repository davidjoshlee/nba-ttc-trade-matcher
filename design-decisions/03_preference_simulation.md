# Design Decision: Preference Simulation

## Decision
Team preferences are automatically generated by analyzing roster composition against an "ideal roster template" and ranking available players by how well they fill identified gaps.

## How it works
1. **Team-specific gap analysis**: Each team's ideal roster composition is adjusted based on their statistical profile. Teams weak in scoring get higher ideal counts for offensive roles (Volume Scorer, Floor Spacer); teams weak in defense prioritize Defensive Anchor and Two-Way Wing. This creates diverse preferences across teams — critical for generating multi-team trade cycles.
2. **Urgency scoring**: (ideal_count - actual_count) / ideal_count. Score of 1.0 = total absence of a needed role. Roles filled by secondary-role players count at 0.5x.
3. **Player ranking**: For each available player on other teams, preference_score = role_urgency × player_quality_score. Rotation Players (no strong archetype) receive a quality-scaled baseline urgency rather than a flat value, so better bench players are meaningfully preferred over worse ones.
4. **Quality score**: Weighted composite of pts, reb, ast, stl, blk, minus turnovers.

## Tradeoff: Realism vs. Tractability
- **What we gain**: Fully automated preferences for all 30 teams. No human input needed to run the demo. Preferences are data-driven, consistent, and diverse enough to drive multi-team trades.
- **What we lose**: Real GMs consider chemistry, age, injury history, contract length, scheme fit, cap flexibility — none of which we model. Our team-specific adjustments are based on aggregate stats, not coaching philosophy or front-office strategy.

## Why not human input?
The proposal mentions managers providing their own preferences. For the prototype, simulated preferences let us demo the full system without needing 30 human inputs. The architecture supports swapping in human preferences — the TTC algorithm only needs a ranked list per team.

## Baseline urgency for Rotation Players
Players classified as "Rotation Player" (no strong archetype) receive a quality-scaled baseline urgency (0.05 to 0.20, proportional to their stat quality) so they can still participate in trades with meaningful differentiation. Without this, the majority of available players would be either excluded from preferences entirely or treated as interchangeable.
