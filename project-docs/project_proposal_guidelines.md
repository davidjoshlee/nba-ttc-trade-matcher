Proposal (1-2 pages)

Your proposal should cover the following:

The problem. What coordination problem does your platform address? Who are the participants, what transaction or interaction does the platform facilitate, and why does the problem matter? Be specific. "A platform for X" is not a problem statement; the problem is what goes wrong without the platform (or what goes wrong with existing platforms).

Platform concept. Describe the core mechanism. How does the platform work at a high level? What is central to your design? Be clear on what matters (matching? pricing?) and why.

Prototype scope. Identify what you plan to build as a working prototype. This should be a functional piece, not a wireframe or a slide deck. Examples: a matching algorithm, a pricing engine, a ranking system, a trust/reputation mechanism. State what tools you will use (Colab, Airtable, Claude/ChatGPT, other) and what the inputs and outputs of your prototype will be.

Design tradeoffs. Identify at least a couple meaningful tradeoffs in your design. These should be genuine tensions. Examples: relevance vs. option diversity in matching, revenue vs. fairness in pricing, simplicity vs. expressiveness in the trust system. For each tradeoff, state which side you are leaning toward and why.

Evaluation plan. How will you know if your prototype works? What does "good" look like for your system, and how will you measure it? Include how you plan to validate the AI's outputs (not just whether the code runs, but whether the decisions are sensible).

Format: 1-2 pages, no appendices. Conciseness counts.

What we are looking for: A clear problem with real stakes, a focused prototype plan, and evidence that you have thought about what makes the design hard. We are not evaluating ambition or polish at this stage. We want to see that your team has a tractable plan and understands the core tradeoffs involved.
